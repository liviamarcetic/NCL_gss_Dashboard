The fleet is improving, 2023 → 2026

Share of comparable general-comment text phrased negatively, by year. Comparable basis only — excludes NCL's 12 dedicated 2026 survey boxes for a fair year-over-year read.
Negative-phrased comments fell every year: 19.1% → 18.0% → 17.4% → 17.1%.
Nearly every category improved on this basis. The one clear exception is Value/Pricing, which moved the wrong way — from 26.0% negative in 2023 to 28.1% in 2026 YTD. See Recommendations below.

Category performance, 2026 YTD

Two different measures — toggle between them. They don't always agree (see the callout below the chart).
% of improvement-comments phrased negatively (0–32% scale). Lower is better. Reflects the tone guests used when writing suggestions — not how satisfied they were.
These two views don't always agree.
Onboard Recreation has a mid-pack % negative (27.0%), but the lowest Avg GSS of any category (3.26). Itinerary/Ports has the highest % negative (27.6%) but a decent Avg GSS (3.67). A category can get plenty of negatively-phrased suggestions from guests who were still, overall, satisfied — and vice versa. Use % Negative to spot where guests have the most complaints to make; use Avg GSS to spot where the guests themselves were least happy.

Biggest gap to close: Bars & Beverage wait times
"Wait Time/Service Speed" comments in Bars & Beverage run 76% negative, and "Ambiance/Noise/Crowding" runs 53% negative — the two most negative sub-themes found anywhere in this dataset at meaningful volume. This is the single most actionable, highest-confidence finding in the workbook.

Top recommendations

Full list and supporting evidence in the source workbook's Recommendations tab.
High priority
Add bar staff / streamline service at peak hours (fleet-wide)
Bars & Beverage "Wait Time/Service Speed" runs 76% negative on 2,657 comments — the highest-confidence negative finding in the dataset. Short-term horizon; likely to lift F&B sentiment broadly.
High priority
Investigate internet/connectivity fleet-wide
"Quality of internet connection" is 32% negative across 19,290 comments — the largest single driver inside Guest Services. Medium-term; likely to lift Guest Services and Information/Communication together.
High priority
Investigate Value/Pricing — the one category trending worse, 2023→2026
Every other category improved over the period; Value/Pricing moved from 26.0% to 28.1% negative. The clearest multi-year warning signal in the data — worth root-causing (pricing changes, package restructuring, gratuity confusion) before it compounds.
Medium priority
Ship-specific review of Itinerary/Ports complaints
Itinerary/Ports has the highest fleet-wide negative share (27.6%); Aqua, Getaway, and Escape run above the fleet average. Confirms whether this is a port/excursion-pricing issue or ship-specific before committing to an itinerary redesign.
Medium priority
Review embarkation/debarkation queue management on Star, Pearl, Dawn
These 3 ships run above fleet-average negative share for this category; wait time and communication clarity are the top recurring themes. Targeted terminal-process fixes rather than fleet-wide investment.
Low priority
Study and replicate what's working on above-average ships
Not every finding is negative — several ships and categories perform meaningfully above the fleet average. Identify the staffing model, layout, or training behind that and consider replicating fleet-wide.
Methodology caveat: This dashboard uses a rule-based text classifier, validated against NCL's own pre-tagged sentiment field in the 2025 data at 58.7% whole-comment agreement. Treat every percentage as directionally reliable, not precise to the decimal — large, consistent multi-year trends (like Value/Pricing) are far more trustworthy than any single-category, single-year number.
